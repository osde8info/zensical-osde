date: 2010-10-13

---

If you have [test driven the IE9](http://ie.microsoft.com/testdrive/ "ie9") HTML 5 web browser and are thinking of installing the [M$ IE 9 32bit beta](http://windows.microsoft.com/en-GB/internet-explorer/download/ie-9/worldwide "ie9") browser or [M$ IE 9 64bit beta](http://windows.microsoft.com/en-GB/internet-explorer/download/ie-9/worldwide "ie9") browser locally then dont fall into the trap of thinking that just installing the 64bit one will leave your IE 8 32bit alone - it doesnt !

After downloading and installing the [M$ IE 9 64bit beta](http://windows.microsoft.com/en-GB/internet-explorer/download/ie-9/worldwide "ie9") I found that when I tried to run my old IE8 32bit browser that in fact the IE9 32bit beta was running instead !

[![ie9 beta browser](http://farm5.static.flickr.com/4065/5078742990_ccdf67b491_m.jpg)](http://www.flickr.com/photos/osde-info/5078742990/ "ie9 beta browser by osde8info, on Flickr")

Update : Instead of installing IE9 Beta you can now download the [IE9 Platform Preview](http://ie.microsoft.com/testdrive/info/downloads/Default.html "ie9 beta") which will still let you use IE8 !
