According to the Star Trek Fan site [memory-alpha](http://memory-alpha.org/en/wiki/BBC) the BBC _was the first-run broadcaster of Star Trek ([12 July](http://memory-alpha.org/en/wiki/12_July "12 July") [1969](http://memory-alpha.org/en/wiki/July_1969 "July 1969")\-[15 December](http://memory-alpha.org/en/wiki/15_December "15 December") [1971](http://memory-alpha.org/en/wiki/December_1971 "December 1971")) and TNG ([26 September](http://memory-alpha.org/en/wiki/26_September "26 September") [1990](http://memory-alpha.org/en/wiki/September_1990 "September 1990")\-[6 May](http://memory-alpha.org/en/wiki/6_May "6 May") [1992](http://memory-alpha.org/en/wiki/May_1992 "May 1992"), up to "[The Best of Both Worlds, Part II](http://memory-alpha.org/en/wiki/The_Best_of_Both_Worlds%2C_Part_II_%28episode%29 "The Best of Both Worlds, Part II (episode)")"). After [1992](http://memory-alpha.org/en/wiki/1992_productions "1992 productions"), the first-run rights of TNG – and later DS9 and_ _Voyager – went to [Sky One](http://memory-alpha.org/en/wiki/Sky_One "Sky One"), with the BBC showing the episodes several months later._
